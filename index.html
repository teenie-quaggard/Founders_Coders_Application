hello

My name is

Kristina

I am a 29-year-old Malay Filipino with a degree in sculpture. In 2012, I graduated from the Emily Carr University of Art and Design in beautiful British Columbia, Canada.

Before moving to London, I lived in Berlin where I worked as the head assistant for an artist named Simon Fujiwara. My job was to research, source and test materials, create artworks and manage the production of large scale exhibitions.

After relocating to London, I felt a pull to direct my energy into something that felt more personally meaningful and I moved into the field of social support. For the past two years, I have been working as a tutor for children with disabilities while also facilitating community engagement projects for the disability charity Mencap.

In addition to my paid employment, I volunteer at Switchboard, the London-based LGBT+ helpline. The helpline is staffed exclusively by LGBT+ individuals who go through a rigorous 6 month training process. We talk about a range of issues including gender, identity, sexuality and mental health.

why
coding

While working at the charity, it was my responsibility to maintain a company blog so that the service users could keep up to date on the types of activities that we had running.

Although the blog was created as a sort of 'community newsletter' for the service users, the styling and accessibility features of the site were so outdated that many of the people I worked with could not navigate the webpage without help. I became interested in finding ways to fix this problem, but wasn't able to with my very limited knowledge of HTML.

The beauty of the world wide web is that it brings people together and spreads information quickly; but, like other public places, the internet needs to be a place that is accessible for many different types of users. I am interested in learning how to code so that I can help bridge a gap that seems to be widening faster than it is closing.

What now?

This is my first webpage and I realise that there is a lot of room for improvement. Moving forward, I am interested in becoming comfortable creating responsive webpages with accessible design and cleaner code.

I am also extremely excited to explore the limits of Javascript.
